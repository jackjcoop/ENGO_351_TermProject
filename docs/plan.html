Tentative Plan

 

  1. Project Ideation and Data Ideation 
    • Overall: 
      1. Our team will begin to ideate different project ideas, and explore different datasets that could be collected. Our team will begin narrow in on ideated project ideas and begin selecting a project scope that is both rich in exploration as well as limited enough that it would be feasible within the scope of the term project. 
    • Acceptance Criteria: 
      1. Group formalized and submitted to the course instructor 
      2. Shortlist of project ideas 
      3. Selection of project idea 
  2. Project Planning    
    • Overall: 
      1. Following the selection of a project theme, in our team’s case, the selection of the theme of “Hurricane Analysis” we will begin to explore potential data sources available for the chosen theme. These data sources will be selected, and the scope of the project will be defined. The initial setup of the webpage will take place and the development of the project plan will also take place. 
    • Acceptance Criteria: 
      1. Project Idea Chosen 
      2. Data-sources has been collected 
      3. The webpage has been submitted 
  3. Data Collection: 
    • Overall: 
      1. Following preliminary data sources being selected, these data sources will begin to be explored and appropriate documentation will be recorded. The initial data exploration will consist of exploring various parameters as well as supplemental external research to inform how this data could be further processed and analyzed in future steps. This supplemental research will be crucial as it will inform what types of analysis will be most fruitful in its exploration. 
    • Acceptance Criteria: 
      1. The table of data sources will be updated to include appropriate documentation 
  4. Data Preparation    
    • Overall: 
      1. Initial analysis of the data will begin to occur such as querying data to explore various existing and potential relationships, utilization of geoprocessing tools to develop heatmaps, charts, and other visualization options. This initial analysis will serve to begin to explore how this data can begin to tell an informative story on the prevalence and extent of hurricanes. 
    • Acceptance Criteria: 
      1. Adding initial results from the processing of data and analysis to the web-page 
  5. Data Processing    
    • Overall: 
      1. Following the initial exploration, plans for further analysis will be developed and these plans will be executed to present desired analytics of the data. Where outlined to be possible, the use of the Model Builder will be used to automate various arduous tasks. Following the analysis, this will then be published to ArcGIS Online for future reference. 
    • Acceptance Criteria: 
      1. Screenshots of the Model Builder Processes
      2. Documentation of the Model Builder Processes 
      3. Final Analyses Presented 
      4. ArcGIS Online links added to the webpage
  6. Presentation    
    • Overall: 
      1. Final adjustments will be made to the presentation of the data. The story map will be created and published in ArcGIS Online. This will then be used for the final presentation of the term project. 
    • Acceptance Criteria: 
      1. Creating a Story Map in ArcGIS Online
      2. Story Map hyper-link included in the webpage
      3. Final edits to the webpage
